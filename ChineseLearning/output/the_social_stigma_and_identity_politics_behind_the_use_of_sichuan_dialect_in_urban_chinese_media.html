    As an important dialect in southwestern China, the Sichuan dialect holds complex social and cultural significance in modern urban media. Although Sichuan dialect is widely used in the daily lives of local people, its use in public media is often considered informal and even carries certain social stigma. This phenomenon reflects the complicated societal attitudes toward dialect speakersâ€™ identity and also reveals the influence of identity politics in language choice.

    In recent years, with the promotion of cultural diversity, the Sichuan dialect has gradually attracted more attention. Films, TV dramas, and online programs have begun to attempt expressing local characteristics through Sichuan dialect, which helps to present Sichuan culture more vividly. However, some media audiences regard Sichuan dialect as â€œrusticâ€ or insufficiently â€œmodern,â€ leading to actors and hosts who speak Sichuan dialect sometimes being stereotyped.     At the social level, Sichuan dialect speakers are often required to use Mandarin in formal occasions because Mandarin is viewed as a more standard and modern language tool. The limited use of Sichuan dialect in public domains causes many people to feel that their dialect is marginalized or even discriminated against. This media tendency invisibly deepens regional cultural divides and creates confusion for Sichuan dialect users in expressing their identity.

    However, language is not only a tool of communication but also an essential part of cultural identity. Many Sichuan dialect speakers hope to express their cultural characteristics and emotions through the media so that their identity can be acknowledged and respected by society. Understanding and accepting the status of Sichuan dialect in urban media helps promote social inclusion and cultural diversity. In the future, as social attitudes change, the portrayal of Sichuan dialect in media may become richer and more respected, thereby reducing social stigma caused by language differences.
