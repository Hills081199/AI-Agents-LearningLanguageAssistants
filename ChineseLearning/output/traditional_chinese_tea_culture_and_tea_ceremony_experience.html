Lesson Plan Outline: Traditional Chinese Tea Culture and Tea Ceremony Experience (HSK 3 Level)

I. Learning Objectives

By the end of the lesson, students will be able to:

1. Understand basic concepts and cultural significance of traditional Chinese tea culture.

2. Recognize and use key vocabulary related to tea and tea ceremonies.

3. Describe simple processes involved in a Chinese tea ceremony using appropriate grammar structures.

4. Express preferences and experiences regarding tea using HSK 3 level sentences.

5. Participate in a guided tea ceremony experience, using learned terms and polite expressions.

    Show Translation

    China has a long history of tea culture. Traditional tea culture is not only about drinking tea but also includes the etiquette of brewing and tasting tea. Tea is an important beverage in China and also a symbol of culture. Chinese people like to use tea to entertain guests, expressing friendliness and respect.

    Tea leaves are the essential materials for brewing tea. When brewing tea, first put the tea leaves into the teapot, then pour hot water over them. After brewing the tea, people use small teacups to taste the tea. When tasting tea, you first smell the fragrance of the tea, then slowly drink it to experience the flavor. There are many kinds of tea leaves, including green tea, black tea, and oolong tea. Each type has a different taste and brewing method.

    In traditional tea etiquette, brewing and serving tea are very careful processes. The person hosting the tea ceremony follows standard steps to brew tea, such as warming the cups first, then putting in the tea leaves, and finally pouring the water. The whole process is quiet and elegant, giving people a feeling of relaxation. Experiencing the tea ceremony not only teaches tea-brewing skills but also allows people to feel the beauty of Chinese culture and its deep history.

    Many foreigners who come to China enjoy participating in a tea ceremony experience. They learn to say words like "teacup" and "tea leaves" in Chinese, brew tea together with the teacher, and feel the charm of tea culture. Through the tea ceremony, people not only learn language knowledge but also deepen their understanding of traditional Chinese culture.
