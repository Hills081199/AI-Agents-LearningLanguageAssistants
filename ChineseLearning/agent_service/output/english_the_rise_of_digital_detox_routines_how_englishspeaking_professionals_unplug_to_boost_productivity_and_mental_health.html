CEFR Level: B2 (Upper-Intermediate)

1. Learning Objectives:

By the end of the lesson, students will be able to:

  • Understand and discuss the concept of digital detox and its impact on productivity and mental health.
  • Use key vocabulary related to digital detox and wellbeing accurately in speaking and writing.
  • Apply the present perfect tense and modal verbs to talk about habits, experiences, and advice associated with digital detox.
  • Develop critical thinking by sharing opinions on technology use and managing digital distractions.

    • 3. Grammar Points:

    a) Present Perfect Tense

  • Focus: Usage to talk about experiences, changes, and actions that have relevance to the present.
  • Example: "Many professionals have started digital detox routines to improve their focus."
  • Practice: Students will form sentences and short paragraphs describing personal experiences with digital devices or attempts to unplug.

    • b) Modal Verbs for Advice and Obligation (should, ought to, must)

  • Focus: Using modals to give advice, make recommendations, and express necessity related to digital detox and mental health.
  • Example: "You should limit your screen time to avoid feeling overwhelmed."
  • Practice: Role-plays and discussions where students give advice to a colleague about managing digital distractions.

    In recent years, more and more English-speaking professionals have started embracing digital detox routines. This new trend has grown as people recognize how constant connections to their devices can cause distractions and affect their mental health. Many professionals have found that these breaks help improve their productivity and overall wellbeing.

    The modern workplace often requires employees to stay online for long hours. However, this connection can become overwhelming. Constant notifications, emails, and social media updates can drain attention and increase stress levels. To counter these effects, individuals have developed habits that limit their screen time. Many have discovered that taking time away from screens improves their sleep, mood, and creativity. Such positive changes contribute directly to better performance at work. In fact, several companies have introduced policies encouraging employees to unplug during breaks or after office hours to boost productivity.

    In conclusion, the rise of digital detox routines reflects a growing awareness among English-speaking professionals about their mental health and productivity. Those who have made digital detox a habit have already noticed the positive effects, and they encourage others to do the same for a healthier, more balanced lifestyle.

    üìê Grammar Points
    Present Perfect Tense (have/has + past participle)
    Used to describe experiences, actions that occurred at an unspecified time in the past but are relevant now, or changes over time.
    Example
    Many professionals have started digital detox routines to improve their focus.
    Modal verbs for advice (should, ought to, must)
    Used to give advice, recommendations, or express necessity or obligation.
    Example
    You should limit your screen time to avoid feeling overwhelmed.
    Use of 'have + past participle' to describe experiences
    This pattern is used to talk about what someone has experienced up to the present moment.
    Example
    They have discovered that taking time away from screens improves sleep.
    Use of 'set + noun phrase' for routines
    The verb 'set' followed by a noun phrase can describe arranging or deciding on specific rules or times.
    Example
    Some people set specific 'no device' times during the day.
    Use of 'allow + object + to + verb' for enabling actions
    Using 'allow' followed by an object and an infinitive verb to indicate permission or enabling.
    Example
    Small changes encourage mindfulness, allowing the brain to rest and refocus.
