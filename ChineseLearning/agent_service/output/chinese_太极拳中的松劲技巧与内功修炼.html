    Tai Chi is a traditional Chinese martial art that emphasizes relaxation of the body and cultivation of internal energy. When practicing Tai Chi, relaxation, known as "songjin," is a very important technique. To relax means to loosen the muscles of the body and avoid tension. Only by doing so can movements become more relaxed and natural, and it can also protect the body from injury. During Tai Chi practice, special attention must be paid to relaxing the strength in the shoulders, arms, and legs, not letting them become stiff. Only when the body is relaxed can one better feel the flow of internal energy and coordinate with breathing.

    The cultivation of internal energy (neigong) is the core part of Tai Chi. It mainly involves coordinating breathing and intention to improve the bodyâ€™s balance and concentration. When practicing neigong, we adjust movements gradually with the breath, achieving the result of smooth and unblocked breathing. Many Tai Chi practitioners feel their movements become more stable, and their minds become calm and relaxed. Neigong cultivation not only strengthens the bodyâ€™s flexibility but also enhances mental focus. Through long-term practice, both body and mind reach a harmonious state.

    In the learning process of Tai Chi, mastering the technique of relaxation and cultivating neigong are very important. Relaxing the bodyâ€™s strength allows it to become more relaxed and flexible; through neigong training, movements can become more stable and breathing smoother. For beginners, understanding and practicing these techniques may be somewhat difficult, but as long as they persist, they will gradually appreciate the mysteries and enjoyment of Tai Chi.
